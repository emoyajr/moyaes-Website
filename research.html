Research Focus:

Developing a production-grade real-time fraud detection system for e-commerce transactions using distributed streaming architecture. The system processes high-velocity transaction streams through Apache Kafka, analyzes patterns using Spark Streaming, and maintains dual-storage architecture with PostgreSQL for historical analysis and Redis for sub-millisecond lookups. The research investigates the effectiveness of combining rule-based detection with statistical anomaly detection to identify fraudulent activity with minimal latency.

Seismicity Modeling with Extreme Value Theory

üìç Bloomington, Indiana üìÖ May 2025 - August 2025

Research Focus:

Applied Extreme Value Theory to quantify seismic hazard in Veracruz State, Mexico, using over a century of earthquake data from the National Seismic Service. The research implemented a dual-modeling framework combining the Generalized Extreme Value Distribution for annual block maxima and the Generalized Pareto Distribution for threshold exceedances. The model revealed that a magnitude 7.0 earthquake has approximately a 12.8% probability of occurring within 50 years.

Key Contributions:

  • Led statistical modeling and diagnostic validation, including threshold selection via mean residual life plots and parameter stability analysis.
  • Conducted comprehensive data preprocessing involving declustering of aftershock sequences and standardization of 6,121 seismic events.
  • Successfully estimated 10, 50, and 100-year return levels, providing actionable insights for civil engineering design standards and emergency preparedness policy in tectonically active regions.

Principal Investigators: Dr. Javier Rojo | Dr. Sergio Juarez
